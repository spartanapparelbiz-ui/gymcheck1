GYMcheck is an AI-powered tool that gives automated feedback on lifting form. It is not a doctor, physical therapist, certified personal trainer, or any other licensed health or fitness professional. Do not use it in place of one.

If you are injured, in pain, or have any medical condition, stop using the Service and consult a qualified healthcare provider immediately.

1. Educational & Informational Use Only

The information, scores, flags, and recommendations generated by GYMcheck are for general educational and informational purposes only. They are based on automated analysis of an uploaded image or video frame and may be inaccurate, incomplete, or inappropriate for your specific situation, body, equipment, or goals.

You should never use GYMcheck output as a substitute for personalized advice from a qualified professional.

2. AI Limitations You Must Understand

  • The AI analyzes a single image or video frame, not a full picture of your training, history, or biomechanics.
  • The AI can be wrong. It may flag issues that don't exist or miss issues that do.
  • The AI does not know your injury history, mobility limitations, age, weight, or training experience.
  • The AI cannot detect every potential injury risk, especially internal or progressive conditions.
  • Camera angle, lighting, and video quality significantly affect accuracy.

If something the AI says contradicts your gut, your body, or a qualified coach — trust them, not the AI.

3. Consult a Doctor First

Before starting any new exercise program, changing your training routine, or acting on any guidance from GYMcheck, you should consult a qualified physician — especially if any of the following apply to you:

  • You have any pre-existing medical condition (heart disease, high blood pressure, diabetes, joint issues, etc.)
  • You are recovering from an injury or surgery
  • You are pregnant, postpartum, or breastfeeding
  • You are over 45 and not currently active
  • You take any medications that affect heart rate, blood pressure, or balance
  • You have any reason to suspect physical activity could harm you

4. Inherent Risks of Weightlifting

Weightlifting and resistance training carry inherent risks. By using the Service to inform your training, you acknowledge that these risks include but are not limited to:

  • Muscle strains, tears, and pulls
  • Joint injuries (knees, shoulders, lower back, elbows, wrists)
  • Herniated or ruptured spinal discs
  • Fractures and broken bones
  • Tendon and ligament damage
  • Cardiovascular events including heart attack and stroke
  • Worsening of pre-existing conditions
  • In rare cases, permanent disability or death

These risks exist regardless of whether you use GYMcheck. Using GYMcheck does not eliminate or significantly reduce these risks.

6. Stop If Anything Hurts

Pain is information. If a movement causes pain, stop. If something feels wrong, listen to your body, not the AI score. Cues that may signal a problem include but are not limited to:

  • Sharp or shooting pain (different from normal muscle fatigue)
  • Joint pain that lingers after the workout
  • Numbness or tingling in any extremity
  • Chest pain, shortness of breath, dizziness, or nausea during training
  • Any unusual or unexpected symptom

If any of these occur, stop immediately and seek medical attention.
